Abstract Crystallins are the dominant structural components of the eye lens. Among the different type of crystallins, the beta and gamma crystallins form a family of related proteins [1, 2]. Structurally, beta and gamma crystallins are composed of two similar domains which, in turn, are each composed of two similar motifs with the two domains connected by a short connecting peptide. Each motif, which is about forty amino acid residues long, is folded in a distinctive 'Greek key' pattern.
